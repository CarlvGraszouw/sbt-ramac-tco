RAMAC™ asset tracking — executive summary

G-Matrix Systems · prepared for SBT · Model period: 60 months (5 years) — align the Years field when comparing to this narrative.

Project 1 — RAMAC™ AT1L · Cash bag embedded tracker

Low-power embedded tracker for covert deployment; passive until required, then recovery via Sigfox triangulation and GPS burst.

  • Location on request · daily heartbeat · recovery-mode burst tracking
  • Up to ~3-year battery (design intent) · sewn into bag (non-accessible → full swap at service)
  • Service: battery + enclosure every 18 months (3 cycles in 5 years)

Project 2 — RAMAC™ AT2 · Teller machine tracker

Powered tracker with backup battery for fixed installs; optional IO and door monitoring.

  • Movement detection · door monitoring (dry contact) · daily heartbeat + diagnostics
  • Backup battery for power loss · optional breadcrumb tracking during movement
  • Service: battery every 18 months; enclosure every 2nd cycle (36 mo) — 3 battery / 1 enclosure events in 5 years

Strategic interpretation: AT1L is a cost-efficient recovery solution for high-volume mobile assets (passive → reactive). AT2 is a security and monitoring platform for high-value fixed assets with proactive alerts to reduce loss risk.

Calculation basis: device purchase is a one-off in Year 1. Sigfox is charged annually per device with escalation applied per contract year (Year n uses (1 + Sigfox %)^(n−1) on the base annual fee). Service events fall in the calendar year of the event month.
